Resend Lost Licence Key
Customer can't find their licence email — deleted it, changed email client, or it went to spam. You click Resend in the admin dashboard. The Worker fetches their current email address live from Stripe and delivers a fresh copy of the exact same key.
● Customer ● You (Admin) ● Worker ● D1 ● Stripe ● Resend Email
Step-by-Step Flow
8 steps. Quick support action — takes about 30 seconds to complete. Click any step for detail.
1
● Customer
Customer contacts you — can't find their licence email
Usually via email to hello@modelcollectr.co.uk. They might say "I deleted the email", "I can't find my key", or "I set up a new PC and lost the key".
Verify they are a paying customer before resending. A quick check: search their email in the Stripe Dashboard under Customers. If their subscription is active, proceed. No formal verification process needed — if they have a Stripe subscription with their email, they're legitimate.
2
● Admin
Open the admin dashboard
Navigate to https://api.modelcollectr.co.uk/admin with the X-Admin-Secret header. All active licences are listed with emails from Stripe.
The email column on each row is fetched live from Stripe when the admin page loads — it always reflects the customer's current Stripe email address. Use the "Active" filter if the customer has an active subscription.
3
● Admin
Find the customer's row and click "Resend"
Identify the correct row by email address. Click the blue Resend button. No confirmation dialog — it's safe to do at any time.
If the customer says their email has changed, you can't update it from here — the email is always read from Stripe. Tell them to update their email in their Stripe customer portal, or update it manually in the Stripe Dashboard → Customer → Edit.
4
● Worker
Dashboard sends POST /admin/licenses/:key/resend
The Worker receives the request and begins the resend process by looking up the licence in D1.
POST /admin/licenses/MC-K8HN-4XRQ-BZTW-7YJP/resend
X-Admin-Secret: MC_ADMIN_3F21F96F91C4DFC7BED4B0E4FC829A36
5
● D1 Database
Worker looks up the stripe_customer_id for this key
The email is not stored in D1 (GDPR design). The Worker fetches the Stripe customer ID from D1, then uses it to get the email from Stripe.
SELECT key, stripe_customer_id
FROM licenses
WHERE key = 'MC-K8HN-4XRQ-BZTW-7YJP'

→ { key: 'MC-K8HN-...', stripe_customer_id: 'cus_1XYZ...' }
6
● Stripe
Worker fetches the customer's current email from Stripe
The email is always pulled live from Stripe — so if the customer updated their email with Stripe, the resend goes to the new address automatically.
GET https://api.stripe.com/v1/customers/cus_1XYZ...

→ {
  "id": "cus_1XYZ...",
  "email": "customer@example.com",
  ...
}
If no email is found on the Stripe customer (unusual), the Worker returns an error and the dashboard shows "Error — no email on Stripe customer". In this case, update the email in Stripe first.
7
● Resend Email
Worker sends the licence email via Resend
The exact same email template as the original delivery — key in large monospace box, 4-step activation instructions, support contact.
POST https://api.resend.com/emails

{
  "from": "ModelCollectr <hello@modelcollectr.co.uk>",
  "to": ["customer@example.com"],
  "subject": "Your ModelCollectr Pro Licence Key",
  "html": "... key: MC-K8HN-4XRQ-BZTW-7YJP ..."
}
The key in the email is the same key they originally received. It's fetched from D1, not re-generated. Their key never changes.
8
● Admin
Dashboard shows "Email resent!" toast — reply to the customer
The Worker returns {ok: true}, the dashboard shows the confirmation toast. Let the customer know the email is on its way.
Reply to the customer's email: "Hi [name], I've resent your licence key to [email]. Please check your spam folder if it doesn't arrive within a few minutes. The subject line is 'Your ModelCollectr Pro Licence Key'. Let me know if you need any help activating it."
